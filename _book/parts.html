A Minimal Book Example

Chapter 3 Parts

The high weight of ironcore forcers results in a greater cost of energy due to the elevated inertia of the system. By contrast, ironless linear motors have been designed to overcome this higher inertial burden due to the weight of the materials used in the forcer. In the latter construction the coils of the forcer are cast in an epoxy resign. This has some obvious benefits in terms of the weight the forcer, resulting in higher acceleration and deceleration of movements. Nonetheless ironless linear motors have some limitations when compared to the ironcore variant, which mainly results in higher force ratings for similar sized ironless variants. In order to achieve similar specifications some limitations of the ironless linear motor have to be overcome. These limitations can be roughly divided into three groups:

  • Materials
    The epoxy resin forcers of ironless linear motors have lower thermal conductivity and structural stiffness as the ironcore variant. This can be overcome by:
    • researching and adopting more thermally conductive epoxy resins. Potentially by using nanoparticles inclusions in the epoxy resin (Fu et al. 2010).
    • improving structural stiffness of the epoxy resin. Resin with a high structural stiffness are a focal field of research in prosthodontics studies and this could potentially be for the ironless forcer design.
  • Design parameters
    The Y beam design is an example of a geometric configuration that improved the efficiency of ironless linear motors. Building on these progresses in design specifications one can:
    • further improve motor geometry and other design parameters by state-of-the-art topological and magnetic flux models.
    • adopt precision manufacturing processes (such as laser metal cutting) to implement design improvements with minimal amounts of variation. Minimizing production errors of linear motor production will increase the efficiency of these systems in real-world applications.
  • Adaptive algorithms
    Computational routines for movement tracking and corrective feedback will help overcome magnetic flux interaction and other parasitic forces that negtively influence the machine’s performance. Such algorithms can be benefit from:
    • increased computational power that match the speed of movement of the system will benefit overall performance.
    • self-supervised machine learning can iteratively learn the best routine for certain movements and environmental conditions within a specific system.

Improvements in all of these categories can improve the systems performance without the need of larger and heavier forcers. Reversely, this also creates opportunities to further reduce the size and weight of linear motors. This would be beneficial for the high precision applications, such as, biomedical devices, surgical tools and high-end optics, that require nano- to micro-scale spatial precision with a very consistent velocity control of movement.
