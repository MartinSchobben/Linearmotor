Case Study “Linearmotor”

Chapter 5 Materials

Researching material with greater heat conductivity and stiffnes for the forcer appear to be a critical aspect that could advance ironless linear motor optimization.

  • Leibniz-Institut für Polymerforschung, Dresden: This insittute has a rsearch group especially decicated to researching the effect of carbon nanostructure inclusion in polymeres on thermal properties. These facilities would be excellentely suited to develop ironless forcers with better heat conducting properties.

5.1 Optimising ironless linear motor design

The proposed consortium will combine state-of-the-art artificial neural networks to optimize design parameters before actual production starts. Artificial neural networks have been proven particularly useful to model non-linear systems and have been successfully applied to topology optimization and heat conduction system design problems.

  • Fraunhofer-Institut für Intelligente Analyse- und Informationssysteme IAIS, Dresden: Has a long history of researching intelligent digital solution and also applying their knowledge to real world problems. In addition, they already have an extensive network that contains educational institutes, start-ups and cooperations, which could supplement the consortium.

  • Celus, München: Is an AI-powered start-up that focusses on electronics engineering processes, including: smart solution for material selection and digital twins of electronic components. There experience could help facilitate the design phase but also construct adaptive feedback algorithms that help regulate movement and compensate for unwanted disruptive forces (e.g. parasitic magnetic forces).

5.2 Testing and Production

After the design phase it is important to have a production line for these newly designed linear motors that can deliver the same level of accuracy as used in the virtual designs. These encompasses micro-tooling and critical quality checks along the production line. This allows real life testing of the devices in laboratory experimental set-ups and trial experiments to test the user’s satisfaction with the devices.

  • Faulhaber Gruppe, Schönaich: Is a manufacturer of high-tech miniature and micro motors, among which linear motors, and they actively research solutions for the prosthetics and exoskeleton market. The decades of experience and the level of accuracy of production will realise the design goals as set-out in the previous step.

  • Ottobock, Duderstadt: Makes products at the end of the production chain for the prosthetics and exoskeleton market. Ottobock regularly performs clinical trials of their products to measure how the actual performance improvement impacts the users of exoskeletons and prosthetics. The outcomes of these trails would feed back in to the design face, thereby closes the circle of this design process.
